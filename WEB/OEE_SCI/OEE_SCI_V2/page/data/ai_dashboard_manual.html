Overview

The AI Intelligence Dashboard is a real-time factory monitoring tool that uses AI/ML algorithms to help production managers identify issues early, prevent machine failures, and optimize OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness).

What is OEE?
OEE measures how effectively your production lines are running.
OEE = Availability × Performance × Quality
A score of 85% or above is considered world-class.

Using Filters

Three filters at the top of the dashboard let you narrow the data to a specific scope. All sections update simultaneously when you change a filter.

  1. All Factory — Shows data across all factories. Select a specific factory to drill down.
  2. All Line — Available after selecting a factory. Filters to a single production line.
  3. All Machine — Available after selecting a line. Filters to a specific machine.
  4. Date Range — Pick a custom date range to analyze historical data (up to 30 days).
Date Range Option Period Use case
Today Current day Real-time monitoring of today's performance
Yesterday Previous day Review previous shift results
Last 7 Days Past 7 days Weekly trend analysis
Last 30 Days Past 30 days Monthly performance review
Custom Range User-defined Targeted analysis of a specific period

Summary Cards

Four cards at the top give you an instant overview of the most important AI-monitored metrics.

Next 4H OEE Forecast

The current OEE value and AI's predicted OEE for the next 4 hours, based on recent trends. Includes a trend badge (Improving / Declining / Stable).

Anomaly Detection

Total number of production lines showing statistically abnormal OEE today. A red "CRITICAL" badge appears when any anomaly is high-severity.

High-Risk Machines

Total number of machines at DANGER (risk ≥ 70) or CAUTION (risk 40–69) level. The badge color indicates the highest active severity: red for DANGER, orange for CAUTION.

Line Health Index (Avg)

Average health score across all lines (0–100), based on the 7-day OEE average. A score below 70 indicates a line needs attention.

Trend Badge Meaning
Improving OEE is trending upward — forecast is above current value
Stable OEE is relatively flat — no significant change expected
Declining OEE is trending downward — intervention may be needed

OEE Trend & AI Forecast

This chart shows historical OEE data alongside AI-generated predictions for the next several hours.

Chart Element Description
Solid line (blue) Actual OEE measurements recorded over the past hours/days
Dashed line (orange) AI forecast — predicted OEE values for the upcoming period
Shaded band 90% confidence interval — the AI is 90% confident the actual value will fall in this range
How to read this: If the dashed forecast line is heading below the 85% target line, consider investigating the root cause now — before OEE actually drops.
Forecast accuracy: The AI forecast is based on statistical patterns from historical data. Unexpected events (machine breakdowns, material shortages) are not reflected in forecasts.

Anomaly Detection

This panel shows production lines where today's OEE is statistically unusual compared to their own historical baseline (last 30 days).

How anomalies are detected: The AI calculates a Z-Score for each line's OEE every hour. A Z-Score of 2.0 or higher means the current OEE is more than 2 standard deviations below the 30-day average — a statistically significant drop.
Severity Z-Score range What it means Recommended action
DANGER Z ≥ 3.0 Extreme OEE drop — very unusual for this line Investigate immediately
WARNING 2.0 ≤ Z < 3.0 Significant OEE drop — below normal range Check within 30 minutes
No anomalies shown? This is good news — all lines are performing within their normal range today.

Line Health Index

The health index summarizes the overall condition of each production line using a simple 0–100 score, based on OEE data from the last 7 days.

Score Status Interpretation
80 – 100 Healthy Line is performing well — no action needed
60 – 79 Fair Some degradation — monitor closely
0 – 59 Poor Significant performance issues — prioritize investigation
How it is calculated: Health score = 7-day average OEE for the line, scaled to a 0–100 index. Lines with consistent high OEE receive high scores; lines with frequent dips or downtime receive lower scores.

Predictive Maintenance

This section ranks machines by their estimated failure risk so maintenance teams can prioritize inspections before breakdowns occur.

Risk Score Risk Level Recommended action
70 – 100 High Risk Schedule maintenance within 24 hours
40 – 69 Medium Risk Plan maintenance within the week
0 – 39 Low Risk Continue normal maintenance schedule
How the risk score is calculated: The AI combines three factors:
  • Downtime frequency (40%): How many downtime events occurred in the last 7 days
  • Downtime rate (35%): Average downtime duration vs. available hours
  • Machine age factor (25%): Time since last recorded maintenance event
Important: The risk score is a predictive estimate based on historical patterns. Always combine this information with physical inspection and operator knowledge.

This live feed displays AI-detected events as they happen — no page refresh needed. Events arrive via a persistent server connection (SSE) and are displayed in reverse chronological order (newest first).

Event type Icon Meaning
OEE Anomaly Detected ⚠️ A production line's OEE has dropped significantly below its baseline today
Active Downtime 🔴 A machine is currently in an unplanned downtime state (not yet resolved)
Maintenance Risk Alert 🔧 A machine's risk score has exceeded the high-risk threshold
Quality Alert 🔬 Quality rate on a line has dropped below the acceptable threshold
Severity badge Color What it means
DANGER Red Requires immediate attention
WARNING Orange Should be reviewed soon
INFO Blue Informational — no urgent action needed
NORMAL Green Situation has returned to normal
Connection status indicator:
  • Green dot + "Connected": Live feed is active and receiving events
  • Orange dot + "Reconnecting...": Connection lost — automatically retrying in 15 seconds
Maximum displayed events: The feed shows the latest 15 events. Older events are automatically removed when new ones arrive.

Production Optimization

This section analyzes OEE data from the last 14 days for every production line and presents prioritized improvement opportunities — so you know exactly where to focus effort for the biggest OEE gain.

Priority Levels

P1 — Critical

OEE gap ≥ 20 percentage points below target. Immediate action required.

P2 — High

OEE gap 10–19 percentage points below target. Address within the week.

P3 — Medium

OEE gap under 10 percentage points. Improvement is beneficial but not urgent.

Reading an Optimization Card

Card element Description
Priority badge (P1/P2/P3) Urgency level — see table above
Trend arrow (↑ / → / ↓) Whether OEE on this line is improving, stable, or declining
Current OEE 14-day average OEE for this line
-X%p below target 85% How far below the 85% world-class target this line currently sits
Current bar Visual representation of current OEE; the vertical line marks the 85% target
Potential bar Estimated OEE if the identified bottleneck is resolved
Bottleneck The OEE component (Availability / Performance / Quality) with the largest gap vs. its target
Avail. / Perf. / Quality 14-day averages for each OEE component; the bottleneck component is highlighted
vs. best line How this line compares to the best-performing line in the factory
Improvement Suggestions Click to expand 2–4 AI-generated action items specific to the bottleneck type and gap size

Bottleneck Types

⚙️ Availability

Machines are running less than the scheduled time. Common causes: unplanned downtime, slow starts, changeovers.

🏃 Performance

Machines run but slower than their rated speed. Common causes: micro-stoppages, speed losses, worn tooling.

🔬 Quality

Too many defective or reworked units. Common causes: material issues, process drift, operator errors.

How "Potential OEE" is calculated: The AI estimates how much OEE would improve if the bottleneck component were brought up to its target level. The potential gain is shown as +X% on the potential bar.

Glossary

OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness)
A measure of manufacturing productivity: OEE = Availability × Performance × Quality. World-class target: 85%.
Availability
The percentage of scheduled production time that a machine is actually running. Target: 90%.
Availability = (Operating Time) / (Planned Production Time)
Performance
How fast a machine runs compared to its theoretical maximum speed. Target: 90%.
Performance = (Actual Output) / (Theoretical Maximum Output)
Quality
The percentage of good units produced without defects or rework. Target: 99%.
Quality = (Good Units) / (Total Units Produced)
Z-Score
A statistical measure of how many standard deviations a value is from the mean. A Z-Score of 2.0 or higher indicates an unusually low OEE value that is unlikely to occur by chance alone.
Baseline (30-day)
The historical average and standard deviation of OEE for a specific line over the past 30 days, used as a reference for anomaly detection.
Risk Score
A composite score (0–100) estimating the probability of machine failure, based on downtime frequency, downtime rate, and time since last maintenance.
Health Index
A 0–100 score summarizing a production line's overall condition based on its 7-day OEE average. Higher is better.
Bottleneck
The OEE component (Availability, Performance, or Quality) that is furthest below its target, limiting overall OEE the most.
SSE (Server-Sent Events)
A technology that allows the server to push real-time updates to the browser over a persistent HTTP connection — used for the live streaming feed.
Confidence Interval (90%)
A range within which the AI is 90% confident the actual OEE value will fall. A wider band means more uncertainty in the forecast.
P1 / P2 / P3
Priority classification for optimization opportunities: P1 (Critical, gap ≥ 20%p), P2 (High, 10–19%p), P3 (Medium, <10%p).

FAQ

Why is the dashboard showing "--" for some values?

The AI modules are still loading data. Values typically populate within 1–3 seconds of the page loading. If they remain blank after 10 seconds, try clicking Refresh.

Why is the streaming feed showing "Reconnecting..."?

The live connection to the server was interrupted. It will automatically reconnect within 15 seconds. If it stays disconnected, refresh the page.

The Production Optimization section shows no cards. Why?

This means all production lines are currently meeting the 85% OEE target — a good result. Optimization cards only appear for lines that are below target.

How often should I check this dashboard?

The dashboard auto-updates continuously, so you can leave it open on a monitor. For proactive management, review the Predictive Maintenance and Production Optimization sections at the start of each shift.

Are the AI suggestions always correct?

AI suggestions are based on statistical patterns and industry best practices. They should be treated as a starting point for investigation — always verify with on-floor inspection and operator experience before taking action.

The "Last 30 Days" filter loads slowly. Is that normal?

Yes. A 30-day query processes a significantly larger dataset than shorter ranges. Loading typically takes 3–5 seconds. If it exceeds 15 seconds, check network connectivity or contact your system administrator.

The High-Risk Machines card shows a number, but the badge says "CAUTION" — is that correct?

Yes. The card value is the total of DANGER + CAUTION machines. If no DANGER machines exist, the badge shows orange CAUTION. The badge turns red DANGER only when at least one machine exceeds a risk score of 70.

What does "vs. best line" mean on an optimization card?

It shows how many OEE percentage points this line is behind the best-performing line in the same factory. This helps benchmark realistic improvement targets based on what is already being achieved in your own plant.
